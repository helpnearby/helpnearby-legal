# General Principles

**Marketplace nature**
Help Nearby operates a digital marketplace connecting Customers and independent Service Providers. We do not provide or supervise the Services ourselves. This Policy governs how **Escrow Payments** and other amounts are handled when a Booking is cancelled, changed, or disputed.

**Transparency**
Where possible, applicable cancellation windows, call‑out fees, and non‑refundable components will be displayed in the Platform at the time of Booking. In the event of any inconsistency, the specific terms shown in the Booking flow will prevail over the general provisions of this Policy.

**Australian Consumer Law**
Nothing in this Policy excludes, restricts, or modifies any rights you may have under the **Australian Consumer Law** (ACL) or other applicable law. Where our obligations under the ACL differ from this Policy, the ACL will prevail to the extent of the inconsistency.

# Key Concepts

For the purposes of this Policy:

* **Booking** means a confirmed Job between a Customer and a Service Provider made through the Platform.
* **Escrow Payment** means the amount paid by or on behalf of the Customer and held under the arrangements described in the Terms until it is released or refunded.
* **Scheduled Start Time** means the time and date for the Job as shown in the Booking details on the Platform.
* **Scheduled Completion Time** means the estimated completion time for the Job as recorded in the Platform.
* **Service Fee** means any fee charged by Help Nearby to the Customer or Service Provider for use of the Platform, as displayed during the Booking process.

# Customer Cancellations

**Cancelling before the Scheduled Start Time**
Customers may cancel a Booking through the Platform prior to the Scheduled Start Time. The consequences will generally be as follows, unless otherwise stated in the Booking flow:

* **Early cancellation** (for example, more than a minimum notice period before the Scheduled Start Time): the Customer may be entitled to a full or partial refund of the Escrow Payment, less any non‑refundable Service Fees or charges disclosed at the time of Booking.
* **Late cancellation** (for example, within a shorter notice period before the Scheduled Start Time): the Service Provider may be entitled to a cancellation or call‑out fee, and only the remaining portion of the Escrow Payment, if any, may be refunded to the Customer.

Specific notice periods and fee structures may vary by Service type and will be displayed in the Platform where applicable.

**Cancelling after the Scheduled Start Time but before work begins**
If the Customer cancels after the Scheduled Start Time but before the Service Provider has begun work, the Service Provider may still be entitled to a reasonable cancellation or call‑out fee to compensate for time and travel. Any remaining portion of the Escrow Payment may be refunded to the Customer.

**Cancelling after work has commenced**
If the Customer cancels after the Service Provider has begun work on the Job, the Customer may be liable for:

* the agreed price for the work already completed; and
* any reasonable costs or expenses incurred by the Service Provider (for example, materials purchased specifically for the Job),

as determined with reference to the Booking details, any Change Orders, and any evidence available through the Platform. Any remaining portion of the Escrow Payment, if any, may be refunded to the Customer.

**Changing the Booking instead of cancelling**
Where the Parties agree, a Booking may be modified via a Change Order instead of being cancelled. The financial impact of the Change Order (for example, additional charges or reductions) will be displayed in the Platform and applied to the Escrow Payment accordingly.

# Service Provider Cancellations

**Before the Scheduled Start Time**
If a Service Provider cancels a Booking before the Scheduled Start Time:

* the Customer will generally be entitled to a refund of the Escrow Payment (excluding any non‑refundable Service Fees displayed at the time of Booking); and
* Help Nearby may, where feasible, assist the Customer in finding an alternative Service Provider.

Repeated cancellations by a Service Provider may affect their ratings and continued access to the Platform.

**After the Scheduled Start Time**
If a Service Provider cancels a Booking after the Scheduled Start Time or fails to attend without reasonable notice, we may treat this as a no‑show by the Service Provider. In such cases, the Customer may be entitled to a refund of some or all of the Escrow Payment, and the Service Provider may not be entitled to any portion of it.

---

# No‑Shows and Access Issues

**Customer no‑show or access not provided**
If a Customer does not provide access to the Job location at the Scheduled Start Time (and has not cancelled in accordance with this Policy):

* the Service Provider may treat the Booking as cancelled by the Customer; and
* the Service Provider may be entitled to a cancellation or call‑out fee, with only the remaining portion of the Escrow Payment, if any, refunded to the Customer.

**Service Provider no‑show**
If a Service Provider fails to attend the Job at the Scheduled Start Time without reasonable notice or justification:

* the Customer may be entitled to a refund of some or all of the Escrow Payment; and
* the Service Provider may not be entitled to any payout for that Booking.

We may also take account‑level action against the Service Provider, including warnings, suspension, or removal from the Platform.

# Safety, Legality, and Unsuitable Conditions

**Unsafe or illegal work**
If a Service Provider reasonably believes that:

* the Job would require them to perform illegal work; or
* the Job site is unsafe or presents an unacceptable risk,

they may refuse to commence or continue the Job. In such cases, the parties should attempt to resolve the issue (for example, by rectifying the unsafe condition or modifying the scope).

**Outcome and refunds**
Where a Job is not completed due to safety, illegality, or unsuitable conditions, Help Nearby may determine, having regard to the circumstances and any evidence, whether any portion of the Escrow Payment should be:

* paid to the Service Provider (for time, travel, or work legitimately performed); and/or
* refunded to the Customer.

# Change Orders and Price Adjustments

**Recording changes**
If the scope of a Job changes, the Customer and Service Provider must record the variation via a Change Order on the Platform. Both parties must agree to the Change Order through the Platform before any additional or reduced work is carried out. This ensures that any additional labour, time, or materials are clearly agreed and reflected in the Escrow Payment.

**Under‑quoted Jobs**
If a Job was significantly under‑quoted based on incomplete or inaccurate information provided by the Customer, the Service Provider may propose a Change Order. If the Customer does not accept the Change Order, the Service Provider may decline to proceed with the Job.

In such cases, Help Nearby may determine whether any portion of the Escrow Payment should be paid to the Service Provider (for time, travel, or partial work) and whether any remaining balance should be refunded to the Customer.

**Work performed without a Change Order**
If a Service Provider performs additional work that is not covered by the original Booking or an approved Change Order recorded on the Platform, Help Nearby cannot guarantee that any additional compensation for that work will be facilitated or recovered through the Platform. Any such additional compensation will be a matter for the Customer and Service Provider to resolve directly.

# Completion of Jobs and Release of Funds

**Manual completion**
A Job may be manually marked as completed when **both** the Customer and the Service Provider indicate Job completion through the Platform. Once both parties have marked the Job as completed, and subject to any holds, checks, or disputes, the Escrow Payment will be released in accordance with section 9 of the Terms.

**Deemed completion after 2 days**
If neither party marks the Job as completed, and no party has notified us of a dispute through the Platform or via our contact details within **2 days** after the Scheduled Completion Time shown in the Platform, the Job will be treated as **deemed completed**.

Upon deemed completion, and subject to any holds, checks, or disputes, the Escrow Payment will be released in accordance with section 9 of the Terms.

**Disputes and holds**
If a dispute is raised before manual or deemed completion, Help Nearby may temporarily place a hold on the Escrow Payment until the dispute is resolved or a determination is made in accordance with section 11 of this Policy.

# Non‑Refundable Amounts

**Service Fees**
Unless otherwise stated, Service Fees charged by Help Nearby for use of the Platform are **non‑refundable**, except where we are required to provide a refund under the Australian Consumer Law or other applicable law.

**Minimum charges and call‑out fees**
Some Bookings may include a minimum charge or call‑out fee as displayed in the Platform. These amounts may remain payable, or non‑refundable, in the case of late cancellations, no‑shows, or access issues, even if the Job is not completed.

**Materials and special orders**
Where a Service Provider has purchased materials or incurred other expenses specifically for a Job and cannot reasonably reuse or return them, the cost of those materials or expenses may be deducted from any refund to the Customer.

# Disputes and Our Role

**Initial resolution between parties**
Customers and Service Providers should first attempt to resolve any disagreement about a cancellation, refund, or Job outcome between themselves using the Platform's messaging features and records.

**Requesting Help Nearby's assistance**
If the parties cannot resolve the matter, either party may contact Help Nearby for limited assistance. We may, at our discretion:

* review relevant Booking details, messages, photos, and other evidence available through the Platform; and
* decide how the Escrow Payment should be split, refunded, or released for the purposes of operating the Platform.

**Nature of our decision**
Any decision we make under this Policy is solely for the operation of the Platform (including how funds held via Stripe Connect are handled). It does **not** constitute legal advice, and it does not prevent either party from pursuing their rights through other means, such as negotiation, mediation, or court proceedings.

# Processing of Refunds

**Method and timing**
Where a Customer is entitled to a refund under this Policy, we will generally process the refund using the same payment method used for the original Escrow Payment.

The time it takes for a refund to appear in the Customer's account will depend on:

* the processing times of Stripe and the Customer's bank or card issuer; and
* any applicable network or banking delays.

**Currency and amounts**
Refunds will usually be processed in Australian dollars (AUD). Any currency conversion fees, bank charges, or differences in exchange rates are the responsibility of the Customer and are not refundable by Help Nearby.

# Chargebacks

If a Customer initiates a chargeback or reversal of a payment with their bank or card issuer, Help Nearby and the Service Provider may be required to provide information to contest the chargeback.

Where a chargeback is upheld:

* the relevant Escrow Payment may be reversed; and
* the Service Provider may be liable to repay any amounts previously paid out in relation to that Booking. Help Nearby may recover such amounts by deducting from future payouts or by other lawful means.
