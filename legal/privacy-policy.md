# What Is Personal Information, and Why Do We Collect It?

Personal Information is information or an opinion that identifies an individual. This Personal Information is obtained in many ways, including:

- name  
- date of birth  
- address  
- email  
- phone number  
- payment information  
- photographs  
- identification documents  
- location information  
- chat messages  
- device details  
- booking and job history  

We don’t guarantee website links or the privacy policies of authorised third parties.

We collect your Personal Information for the primary purpose of providing our services to you, providing information to our clients, and marketing. We may also use your Personal Information for secondary purposes closely related to the primary purpose, in circumstances where you would reasonably expect such use or disclosure. You may unsubscribe from our mailing/marketing lists at any time by contacting us in writing.

When we collect Personal Information we will, where appropriate and where possible, explain to you why we are collecting the information and how we plan to use it.

# Cookies Rejection Option

You can choose to refuse or disable cookies by adjusting your browser or device settings. Most browsers allow you to:

- block all cookies  
- block third-party cookies  
- delete existing cookies  
- receive a warning before cookies are stored  

Please note that disabling cookies may affect certain functionality of our website, including login sessions, saved preferences, and performance features.

Instructions for managing cookies can be found in your browser’s help menu. Examples:

- **Chrome:** Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and Other Site Data  
- **Safari:** Preferences → Privacy  
- **Firefox:** Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data

# Data Breach Notification (NDB Scheme)

We comply with the requirements of the Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) scheme under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth).

If a data breach occurs that is likely to result in serious harm to affected individuals, we will:

- promptly investigate the incident  
- take all reasonable steps to contain the breach  
- notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) as required  

Notifications will include:

- the nature of the breach  
- the information involved  
- steps you should take  
- the actions we have taken to minimise harm

# Data Retention

We retain Personal Information only as long as needed for our functions or to meet legal obligations. Typical retention periods include:

- job/booking records: 7 years  
- ID and license documents: until verification + required retention periods  
- chat data: retained for safety and auditing purposes  
- photos attached to receipts: retained permanently as part of the permanent transaction record required for accounting, compliance, and dispute resolution  

When data is no longer required, it is securely destroyed or de-identified.
